## User Story 1: Adaptive Progression (High Scores)

> **"As a learner with ASD, I want activities that match my ability so that I can continue learning effectively."**

**Scenario:** Learner gets high scores.

![User Story 1: Learner gets high scores](USER%20STORY%201.png)

#### Why 80% Accuracy? 
The 80% threshold for advancing difficulty is deeply rooted in special education and cognitive science, specifically in **Applied Behavior Analysis (ABA) Practices**:
* In ABA therapies and trial-by-trial monitoring systems designed for learners with ASD, mastery criteria commonly sit around the 80% accuracy mark. Setting the accuracy threshold here ensures the skill has been acquired while leaving a 20% margin for natural behavioral variability, attention lapses, or simple motor errors, which are expected and normal.

**Reference:**
Arneliza, A., Sutadi, R., Alsa, A., & Yunanto, K. T. (2026). Development and Implementation of Smart Measurement and Evaluation (Smart ME): A Trial-by-Trial Monitoring System for Autism Therapy. *Journal of Culture and Values in Education, 9*(1), 106-124. https://doi.org/10.46303/jcve.2026.5
